# Tier 2 Source Extraction Guide

This file defines what to extract from each Tier 2 public or approved external source.
Tier 2 sources are independent of the client but may have varying completeness and recency.

---

## Company Registrar Database Record

What to extract:

Official legal name exactly as registered. Any previous names and the dates of name
changes. The registration number. The jurisdiction of the registry. The registered address
as currently filed. The date of incorporation. The current status: active, dormant, struck
off, in liquidation, dissolved.

Current directors: full name, date of appointment, date of resignation if applicable. Flag
any director who appears in the client's Orbis record or UBO declaration but is absent from
the registrar, and vice versa.

Charges and mortgages: any charges registered against the entity, the charge holder, the
date registered, and whether the charge has been satisfied. A registered charge not
previously recorded at the last review is a material change.

Filing history: whether the most recent confirmation statement has been filed on time. A
late or unfiled confirmation statement is a compliance signal. For limited companies,
whether accounts have been filed on time.

Dissolution or insolvency notices: any winding-up petition, liquidation notice, or
administration filing. Always a blocking material change.

Record the database used, the date of the search, and the URL or reference of the record
so it can be traced.

---

## Stock Exchange Filing or Announcement

Apply only to publicly listed client entities. For unlisted entities record not applicable.

What to extract:

The exchange and the filing category. The date of the filing or announcement.

For major shareholding notices: the shareholder name, the percentage held, and whether
the holding has crossed above or below a disclosure threshold. Compare against prior review
to identify new or changed major shareholders not previously recorded.

For director change announcements: the director name, whether the change is an appointment
or resignation, and the effective date.

For profit warnings or financial guidance revisions: the nature of the revision, the
direction (downward or upward), and the reason stated.

For disclosed regulatory investigations or enforcement actions: the regulator named, the
nature of the investigation, and the current status.

For auditor changes: the outgoing auditor, the incoming auditor, and any reason given.

For going concern or audit qualification disclosures: extract the exact nature of the
qualification or going concern statement. Always a material adverse signal.

Record the exchange, the filing reference number, and the filing date.

## News and Media Site Article

Record all news and media findings as signals, not confirmed facts.

What to extract:

The publication name and date. The headline and a one-sentence factual summary of the
content. The nature of the adverse signal: financial crime allegation, regulatory action,
sanctions-related reporting, fraud allegation, corruption allegation, reputational issue,
business distress signal.

Whether the adverse signal has been confirmed by an official source (a regulator's
announcement, a court judgment, a stock exchange filing). If confirmed by an official
source, record the official source as the primary finding and the news article as
corroborating.

Whether the subject of the article is the client entity, a UBO, a director, or an
associated individual.

Whether this finding was present at the prior review or is new.

Do not reproduce article text. Summarise the nature of the signal in one sentence. Do not
treat a news article as a confirmed adverse finding without official corroboration.

---

## Internal Watchlist or Approved Database Match

What to extract:

The name of the list or database. The matched name and match basis (exact, alias, partial).
The date of the match. The nature of the match: what category of risk the list covers.
Whether the match is a confirmed entry or a possible match requiring review.

Whether this match was present at the prior review or is new.

Any notes or analyst commentary attached to the match.

An internal watchlist match always triggers further investigation regardless of what
other sources show. Record the match and flag for reviewer attention. Do not dismiss or
downgrade an internal watchlist match based on the absence of corroborating external
findings.
